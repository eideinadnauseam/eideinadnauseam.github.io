  • Rocky Horror Picture Show (1975)

    • The absolute classic musical that I fully believe no person can truly say they do not like and enjoy some aspect of, if you happen to be this kind of person, you probably should just stop here this website is not going to be for you. As I fully belive it is a universal adored movie, its low weirdness rating, but somehow there are still people out there who have not seen it. Unfortunately with the rise of the far right, these type of boring people probably are the type to not like it. To best sum up the movie without spoiling the antici... pation. The movie is about a young couple on their way to visit a friend and after getting a flat tire enter into a world of gender nonconformity and honesty. In the end the story in my opinion is a calling to how you should not be afraid to be yourself and embrace your weirdness, as a part that should be celebrated and not shamed, which is why it is the perfect movie to celebrate the first post on this website. Are you ready to no longer dream it and be it? If so it is time to do the Time Warp again.

  • Little Shop of Horrors (1986)

    • Based on the classic low budget horror B-movie from the 1960s this movie is a musical comedy that is a must watch for anyone who longs to escape their current world at times or has been disillusioned and has woken up from their American Dream. It follows the story of a orphaned florist who gains popularity and success after his strange plant becomes a sensation. Like Rocky Horror, I fully belive it is a must for most people, and if you have not seen it and I highly recommend it. What would you do for success? For the love of your life? How much blood, sweat, and tears, are you willing to give up for your dreams? If that number is greater than an arm, a leg, or your sense of self, then this mean green mother of a movie will be a perfect fit for you.

  • Hedwig and the Angry Inch (2001)

    • A movie that is a must for anyone who has ever felt like they were not enough or missing a part of themselves in a world that often feels uncaring. When you have a self divided in two like Germany in the later half of the 20th century, how do you find a way to feel whole? Do you pent up your anger and resentment into toxic habits and clinging on to the past or do you find a way to express it and let it out? Do not let other people tell you who you are, and do not let them tell you what you should be, you should be who you want to be and express yourself in a way that is true to you.

  • Phantom of the Paradise (1974)

    • Taking from the Phantom of the Opera and a bit of Faust, this movie is for anyone who ever felt like they have been wronged or taken advantage of by the world. Like Little Shop of Horrors, how much are you willing to give up for your dreams and your story to be shared? Would you be willing to lose your beauty, your own voice, your identity, and your sense of respect in the world to have that story told? If so, here is the blood contract to stardom, but be careful what you wish for, you don't always get what you want and can't go back after this.

  • South Pacific (1958)

    • The timeless musical that is suprisingly still relevant, and holds up, today, exploring themes of love, war, comradery, and cultural differences, set in the South Pacific during World War II. Have you fully looked at yourself in the mirror examining your own prejudices and biases and asking what do you stand for in life? Have you been carefuly tauaght to not judge people by their outsides and by the merit of who they actualy are? If not, how did you get passed the home page? I remember first listening to the vinyl record that I was given in a collection by my grandmother when I was a child humming along and watching the movie made me challenge the preconceived notions of the enviornment I was raised in from rural Colorado.

I feel like, if you were to ask someone what their favorite song from these movies was you would get a pretty good BuzzFeed style personality quiz. Also, I can talk at length about each of these films so more to be discussed in the curiosities section and posibly here with more details to come.
